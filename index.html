Executive Summary

The landscape of LLM-assisted coding has specialized. While earlier models aimed for general proficiency, the latest iterations—Kimi K2.5, MiniMax2.5, GLM-5, and Qwen3.5:397b—exhibit distinct architectural biases that make them exceptionally suited for specific development tasks.

Qwen3.5:397b dominates in raw capability across multiple languages due to its sheer parameter count. GLM-5 stands out in deep algorithmic logic and complex systemic refactoring. Kimi K2.5 leverages its massive context window to become the ultimate repository-level debugger. MiniMax2.5, utilizing an efficient MoE (Mixture of Experts) approach, offers the highest speed-to-accuracy ratio for agile, everyday scripting and frontend development.
